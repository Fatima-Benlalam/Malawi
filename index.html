Lake Malawi

Lake Malawi is an African Great Lake and the southernmost lake in the East African Rift System, located between Malawi, Tanzania and Mozambique. It is the ninth largest lake in the world and third largest and second deepest lake in Malawi.

Liwonde National Park

Liwonde National Park, located in southern Malawi, is one of the country’s most popular wildlife destinations. The park is renowned for its beautiful riverine setting along the Shire River, which enhances wildlife viewing opportunities. Visitors can enjoy boat safaris, as well as traditional game drives and walking safaris. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including elephants, hippos, crocodiles, and recently reintroduced predators such as lions, cheetahs, and wild dogs. Bird enthusiasts will also find Liwonde a paradise, with species like the fish eagle and Pel’s fishing owl frequently spotted

Mount Mulanje

Mount Mulanje, also known as the Mulanje Massif, is a large inselberg in southern Malawi. It is the highest point in the country, with Sapitwa Peak reaching an elevation of 3,002 meters (9,849 feet). The massif covers an area of approximately 650 square kilometers and features a diverse landscape of rolling grasslands, deep ravines, and forested slopes. The mountain is popular for hiking and climbing, offering trails that range from gentle walks to challenging ascents. The unique flora and fauna, including the endangered Mulanje cypress and various endemic species, add to the mountain’s allure3

The Food

Nsima

Nsima is the staple food in Malawi. It's a thick porridge made out of maize flour and water. Typically, nsima is eaten with vegetables and a protein—like fish, beans, or meat. it is popolary eaten with a fish called Chambo

The types of people in Malawi
Malawi is home to several ethnic groups, each with its own language and cultural traditions. The major tribes include:
  • - Chewa:The largest ethnic group, primarily in the central region, known for their rich cultural heritage, including the famous Gule Wamkulu masked dance.

    - Yao: Predominantly found in the southern region, many of the Yao people practice Islam and have a long history of trade and interaction with coastal traders.

    - Tumbuka: Inhabit the northern region and are known for their deep-rooted traditions in education and farming.

    - Ngoni: Descendants of Zulu warriors, they settled in central and northern li strong emphasis

    - Lomwe: Located primarily in the southern region, the Lomwe people have a rich oral tradition and cultural ceremonies.

    - Sena: Found along the Lower Shire Valley, the Sena are known for their agricultural practices, particularly rice farming.
